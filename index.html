NYC Zoning Lots

This site presents a nascent dataset of NYC Zoning Lots compiled from public sources. Click a zoning lot to view more information about its component tax lots, its date of creation, and to preview its associated ACRIS document.

The data presented here is incomplete and may contain inaccuracies and errors. Use the information at your own risk.

What's a Zoning Lot?

About the Dataset

Zoning Lots play an important role in the regulation of land use, but a centralized and accessible database does not exist to track them. We assembled a "best effort" spatial dataset by combining ACRIS records with geometries from MapPLUTO.

The dataset is versioned using the Qri Dataset Version Control System, so you can reliably track changes as we make improvements to the data.

What's Included?

While there are many documents that describe zoning lots in ACRIS, the most predictable and easy to find are filed under type "certificate". We noticed that records of type certificate with two or more tax lots associated usually ended up being zoning lot certification documents

We identified 4,531 ACRIS documents that met our criteria, then used the latest MapPLUTO data to find geometries for the associated tax lots. Of these, 2202 (49%) matched all geometries, 1655 (36%) matched some, and 674 matched none (these are not shown on this map but are included in the dataset).

It's far from a complete picture, but enough to start a conversation about how we can get there.

Caveats
  • There are some documents in this dataset that are not related to zoning lots at all but met the criteria for inclusion.
  • There are many more zoning lots that would not show up under our initial ACRIS Query.
  • All geometries are from the latest version of MapPLUTO. Tax lot boundaries change over time, so a matching geometry may not represent what you see in the scanned document
  • Zoning Lots also change over time, and can be superseded. You may see multiple zoning lots created at different times that overlap
What's next?

Here are some ideas about what to do next

  • Add "flagging" and comments so users can identify bad data. Volunteers can clear these flags and improve the dataset over time.
  • Allow for user to drop a pin where they know a zoning lot is missing. Volunteers can conduct research and add missing zoning lots
